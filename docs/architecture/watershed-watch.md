## Fit verdict

- **Builds on DocketClock:** `yes-partially`
- **Is it a real vertical wedge (not just a skin)?** `True`

Watershed Watch should rent DocketClock for the federal-rulemaking slice of its feed (Regulations.gov dockets + Federal Register notices scoped to a basin) and NOT reimplement reconciliation, but it cannot be "yes-fully" because its single most geo-bearing, deadline-bearing source — the EPA EIS database with its fixed 45-day-draft / 30-day-final clock — is explicitly NOT a DocketClock core source. The architecture lists window_type enum values eis_draft|eis_final ("Typed so EIS-clock verticals can layer on without core changes") but the source adapters are only FR API, Regs.gov v4, GovInfo (link-only), and Mirrulations (offline). So DocketClock gives Watershed Watch the schema slot for EIS but no EIS ingestion, no reconciliation of EIS notices, and no confidence flagging for them. That is the bulk of Watershed Watch's emotional value and it lives outside the substrate today. The honest read: build the federal-docket portion ON DocketClock via the OCD-ID + tag/enrichment contract exactly as architected; build the EPA EIS source, the geo-scoping, and Open States v3 as Watershed Watch's own pipeline. Hence partial. Going standalone would mean re-paying for reconciliation/confidence/provenance the architecture already solved well; going fully-on would require either pushing EIS into the substrate (which violates the federal-rulemaking-first, vertical-agnostic discipline) or pretending EIS doesn't matter (which guts the wedge). Partial is the truthful boundary.

**Wedge reasoning:** Watershed Watch is a genuine instance of the vertical-wedge pattern, but it is a CONSERVATION/environmental wedge, not strictly a "land-use" wedge — and the dossier's self-critique ("just DocketClock + a Waterkeeper skin") must be confronted, not waved away. It is NOT just a skin IF and ONLY IF it earns three things the bare registry provably cannot: (1) Geo-recall — turning "this docket mentions the Choptank / HUC-8 02060005" into a first-class scoping decision. The architecture is explicit that "geo recall is Watershed Watch's problem, not the substrate's"; the substrate "never imports/queries WBD." So resolving basin membership from docket text is net-new vertical IP, not a re-skin. (2) The EPA EIS source + its fixed comment clock, which the substrate does not ingest at all. (3) The action/receipt loop — plain-language brief, structured-evidence comment composer that files to Regulations.gov, honest procedural receipts (filed-on-time, comment counts, draft-to-final diffs, never causal) — which the architecture explicitly classes as "vertical action layer, not substrate" and defers (Pol.is, comment submission, final-rule impact all deferred from core). Where the "skin" accusation has teeth: if Watershed Watch shipped ONLY basin-filtered federal dockets with no EIS source, no geo-recall LLM scoping, and no action loop — i.e. GET /windows?tags=huc_8:X wrapped in green branding — then it IS a skin and should not be funded as a separate product. The dossier's own residual-risk note is correct that the highest-emotion fights (local floodplain rezoning) are exactly what a federal-first substrate can LEAST surface, so the wedge must be honest that it covers federal/state environmental review well and local land-use poorly (NoticeNail community-signal, deferred). It qualifies as a land-use-adjacent wedge because EIS/NEPA review and basin-scoped permitting ARE land/water-use decisions; it is a real wedge because geo-recall + EIS + action are substantive additions; it is at RISK of being a skin precisely on its weakest (local) flank.

### Layer boundary

**DocketClock provides (rented):**
- Reconciled federal ParticipationWindow objects (Regs.gov dockets + FR notices) with resolved_close_utc, resolved_close_display, status, version, change_history
- Stable OCD-IDs as the cross-system join key Watershed Watch keys on (never internal UUIDs) so a basin watchlist survives extensions/reopenings
- The confidence/conflict model (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW/CONFLICTING/STALE/UNKNOWN) + typed conflict_flags + GET /conflicts proof feed — the honest 'do not publish fake certainty' layer Watershed Watch licenses sideways to newsrooms/nonprofits
- Webhooks (HMAC, document-level docket fan-out, conflict-dual-fire, undelivered visibility), RSS/ICS/CSV delivery surfaces
- govinfo_url + disclaimer on every record (legal-reliance backstop so Watershed Watch never claims to be authority)
- window_type enum already including eis_draft|eis_final as the schema slot for EIS windows (slot only, not ingestion)
- The opaque tags array + post-reconcile enrichment hook into which Watershed Watch writes huc_8:/huc_12: tags WITHOUT touching reconciliation
- Extension/correction/reopening reconciliation so a basin alert reflects the OPERATIVE deadline, not a stale original

**Watershed Watch owns (the anti-skin IP):**
- Geo-scoping: USGS WBD (HUC-8/12) via The National Map ArcGIS REST as a VERTICAL-ONLY source; basin picker; resolve intersecting states/counties — the substrate is explicitly WBD-agnostic
- Geo-recall: RuleBox-style LLM rules that decide whether a docket's text/CFR-parts/named water bodies fall in the chosen basin (the hard, net-new recall problem the substrate disclaims)
- The EPA EIS database adapter + its fixed 45-day-draft / 30-day-final clock — NOT a DocketClock core source; Watershed Watch must ingest, normalize, and (ideally) push these through DocketClock's reconciliation contract or its own equivalent
- Open States v3 state environmental legislation (state/local is a substrate thou-shalt-not until funded)
- The action/receipt loop: plain-language basin brief, structured-evidence comment composer filing to Regulations.gov, honest procedural receipts (filed-on-time, comment counts, draft-to-final diffs — never causal)
- Member-reported local long tail (NoticeNail photo-of-notice, parcel geocode) labeled 'community signal, not comprehensive' — DEFERRED past MVP
- Coalition/channel distribution + business model: foundation/EJ grants fund free Tier-1, paid org seats to staffed land trusts/coalitions, the Chesapeake/Waterkeeper design-partner relationship
- Pol.is consensus reserved for genuinely contested multi-faction fights (deferred)

### What the wedge forces the substrate to expose
- A third-party/vertical-writable enrichment hook registry with allowed_tag_namespaces — currently DEFERRED in the MVP boundary ('follows when Watershed Watch commits'). Watershed Watch's existence is the trigger to build it; until then only built-in agency/CFR enrichment exists.
- Tag-based filtering on GET /windows (filter by tags including huc_8:/huc_12:) and tag-scoped webhook subscriptions/watchlists — so a basin watch fans out only to in-basin documents. The architecture lists tags as a /windows filter, which is sufficient IF webhook fan-out also honors tag filters (must be confirmed, not assumed).
- An ingestion path or contract for EPA EIS notices to become first-class windows of window_type eis_draft|eis_final. This is the design-changing ask: either (a) DocketClock adds an EIS source adapter funded by the Watershed Watch pilot (consistent with its 'fund a specific source tranche' rule), or (b) DocketClock exposes a way for a vertical to PUSH externally-fetched notices into the observation log / enrichment so they get OCD-IDs, confidence, and reconciliation. Today neither exists — the enum slot is present but inert.
- Agency-slug and CFR-part tags exposed (already built-in) so Watershed Watch's geo-recall can use agency (EPA/USACE/NPS/BLM) and CFR-part as cheap pre-filters before LLM basin scoping.
- Confidence/conflict_flags surfaced in every API/webhook payload (already contract) so Watershed Watch can downgrade or suppress basin alerts honestly and so the sideways confidence feed to newsrooms is licensable.
- resolved_close_display preserved verbatim (already designed) so basin briefs show legally-correct timezone/channel language, not a collapsed UTC stamp.
- Net effect on substrate design: the vertical does NOT change core reconciliation, but it DOES pull forward two deferred items (third-party enrichment registry; an EIS ingestion contract) from 'later' to 'when Chesapeake commits' — and it stress-tests whether webhook fan-out respects tags, not just the REST filter.

### When to build standalone instead
Build Watershed Watch standalone (not on DocketClock) when ANY of these hold. (1) DocketClock does not exist yet at pilot time and the user's real Chesapeake/Waterkeeper design-partner access creates a now-or-never window — the architecture itself orders Watershed Watch as a FAST-FOLLOW pursued only AFTER the first B2B substrate contract, so if a chapter commits before the substrate is live, a thin standalone (EIS + Regs.gov + FR fetched directly, basin-scoped, with the action loop) ships faster and the user should NOT block a funded pilot on substrate readiness. (2) The federal-docket portion turns out to be a small fraction of in-basin activity versus EPA EIS + state notices — i.e. most value comes from sources DocketClock does not carry — making the substrate dependency mostly overhead. (3) Reconciliation/confidence proves to matter little for the conservation persona: volunteer groups acting on a 45-day EIS clock may not need the extension/correction/timezone reconciliation that liability-bearing reg-affairs buyers do; if conflict cases are rare in environmental dockets (a Week-1-measurable question), the substrate's marquee value is muted for this vertical. (4) Coupling risk: if depending on a single-founder pre-revenue substrate would jeopardize a grant-funded commitment to a coalition, standalone de-risks delivery. The synthesis: the DEFAULT is build-on (partially) to avoid re-paying for reconciliation and to keep the OCD-ID join + sideways confidence-feed revenue; go standalone ONLY if design-partner timing outruns the substrate or if measurement shows federal-docket reconciliation is a minor share of basin value. Even standalone, preserve the OCD-Event output shape so it can later join back to DocketClock — never fork the data model, only the deployment.

### Fit risks
- 'Waterkeeper skin' failure mode: if MVP ships only basin-filtered federal dockets without EIS ingestion, geo-recall, and the action loop, it is provably just GET /windows?tags=huc_8:X re-branded — and the dossier's own residual-risk note becomes the verdict. The wedge must lead with EIS + action + geo-recall, not federal-docket filtering.
- EIS gap: the substrate does NOT ingest EPA EIS (only an inert window_type slot exists). Watershed Watch must build/fund this; if it instead leans on federal Regs.gov dockets alone, it misses the most basin-relevant, deadline-bearing stream and underwhelms exactly where the mission is strongest.
- Geo-recall accuracy: deciding whether a docket falls in a HUC from text is hard; false negatives = missed fights (catastrophic for trust with a passionate base), false positives = noise/burnout. The keyword_false_positive conflict_flag exists in the substrate but basin scoping is the vertical's own unsolved recall problem.
- Local long-tail mismatch: the most emotionally charged fights (local floodplain rezoning, parcel-level permits) are state/local — a substrate thou-shalt-not and a vertical NoticeNail deferral. Volunteers may judge the product by exactly the fights it can LEAST surface.
- Ordering/coupling risk: architecture gates Watershed Watch behind the first B2B substrate contract; the user's live design-partner access may arrive first, forcing either a premature substrate dependency or a standalone fork — manage explicitly so a fundable pilot is not blocked.
- Deferred-dependency risk: the third-party enrichment hook registry and tag-scoped webhook fan-out are not in the substrate MVP; Watershed Watch cannot ship cleanly until they exist, so committing a chapter pulls unplanned substrate work forward.
- Causal-impact temptation: passionate users and grant funders want 'you changed this outcome'; the honest-receipts discipline (filed-on-time, comment counts, draft-to-final diffs only) must hold or the product loses the provenance credibility that is the whole house's moat.
- Channel-economics risk: free grant-funded Tier-1 for volunteers + paid org seats must actually fund the EIS/geo pipeline; if grants are the only revenue, the vertical may not 'earn distribution the bare registry can't,' which is the bar the dossier sets for it to exist.

## Watershed Watch: A Basin-Scoped Environmental Deadline-and-Action Radar (Thick Vertical, Selective Reuse of DocketClock)

A HUC-scoped environmental participation radar for volunteer conservation coalitions that owns the three things the bare registry provably cannot — EPA EIS clock management, basin geo-recall, and an honest monitor→act→receipt loop — while renting DocketClock's reconciled federal-rulemaking windows (Regs.gov + FR) via the OCD-ID + tag/enrichment contract, never reimplementing reconciliation.

## Architecture thesis

Watershed Watch is a THICK VERTICAL with SELECTIVE REUSE — the fit-analysis verdict ('yes-partially') is the correct and only honest layer boundary, and the thick proposal's 6.5 beats the thin proposal's 6.0 for one structural reason: the thin framing treats DocketClock as 'the feed and everything rides on it,' which makes the EIS gap and the deferred enrichment registry into existential single points of failure; the thick framing treats DocketClock as ONE upstream source among three (DocketClock-federal, EPA-EIS-owned, Open-States-owned), which is what the source topology actually is. The 'just a Waterkeeper skin' critique is answered not by branding or by listing three components, but by a single FALSIFIABLE anti-skin test run BEFORE the pilot commitment: 'Can we show the partner a quarter of in-basin EIS windows plus extension-corrected EIS deadlines that GET /windows?tags=huc_8:X cannot produce, AND a measured basin-recall number on a labeled Chesapeake set?' If that test fails, the product is a skin and should not ship. The architecture earns distribution the registry cannot in three ways the registry is structurally barred from: (1) EIS ingestion + an EIS-vs-FR extension reconciliation layer — the substrate is federal-rulemaking-first and vertical-agnostic by discipline, so it will never carry EIS; (2) geo-recall, turning 'this docket touches HUC-8 02060005' into a first-class scoping decision the substrate explicitly disclaims ('geo recall is the vertical's problem'); (3) the action/receipt loop the substrate classes as 'vertical action layer, not substrate' and defers entirely. But the synthesis also absorbs every critique that scored 'fatal' or 'high' and converts them into hard gates: the two unverified kill-shots (Regulations.gov POST /comments availability post-Aug-2025; EPA EIS machine-readability) become Week-1 one-day spikes with named fallbacks, NOT one-line gotchas; the EIS-vs-FR extension reconciliation becomes an explicit owned component (the thick proposal's single worst under-specification); the EIS action/receipt degradation (link-out vs Regs.gov submission ID) is surfaced honestly in the UI as a known second-class receipt; and the value-density question (expected novel in-basin Tier-1 windows/month for a Chesapeake HUC-8) becomes a measured pre-commitment number, because if it is 2-3/quarter the paid-seat thesis is dead regardless of how clean the architecture is. Federal/EIS coverage is honest and strong; local land-use (the most emotionally salient fights) is honestly weak and labeled so — the coverage-tier system is the load-bearing trust device, not a disclaimer footnote.

## Relationship to DocketClock

Partial build-on, not full — and structured so Watershed Watch (WW) survives DocketClock not being live. WHAT WW CONSUMES FROM DOCKETCLOCK (rents, never reimplements): reconciled federal-rulemaking ParticipationWindows (Regs.gov dockets + FR notices) with resolved_close_utc + verbatim resolved_close_display, the full confidence/conflict model (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW/CONFLICTING/STALE/UNKNOWN) + typed conflict_flags + the conflict-dual-fire webhook behavior, stable OCD-IDs as the durable cross-system join key (so a basin watch survives extensions/reopenings without WW tracking the chain), govinfo_url as the legal-reliance backstop, and built-in agency-slug + CFR-part tags used as cheap pre-filters before LLM basin scoping. WHAT WW OWNS (the anti-skin IP, never in the substrate): (a) the EPA EIS adapter + its 45/30-day statutory clock AND its EIS-vs-FR extension reconciliation — DocketClock carries only an inert window_type=eis_draft|eis_final enum slot, no ingestion, no EIS reconciliation, no EIS confidence; (b) USGS WBD/HUC geo-scoping (substrate is WBD-agnostic by design and never imports/queries WBD); (c) the basin-relevance classifier (geo-recall — the substrate explicitly disclaims this as 'the vertical's problem'); (d) Open States v3 state environmental legislation (state/local is a substrate thou-shalt-not); (e) the monitor→act→receipt loop and coverage-tier labeling (substrate classes these as 'vertical action layer, not substrate'). THE SEAM: WW's enrichment worker writes huc_8:/huc_12: tags into DocketClock within an agreed allowed_tag_namespace; corrections to deadlines NEVER flow back through tags — they route through DocketClock's human_review observation path, preserving the substrate's audit invariant. TWO DEFERRED SUBSTRATE ITEMS WW PULLS FORWARD when a Chesapeake chapter commits (this is exactly the substrate's 'paying customer funds a specific tranche' trigger): the third-party/vertical-writable enrichment hook registry, and CONFIRMED tag-scoped webhook fan-out (the substrate lists tags as a /windows REST filter but does NOT confirm webhooks honor tag scopes — this must be verified, not assumed). DEGRADED MODE until those ship: WW polls GET /windows filtered by agency (EPA/USACE/NPS/BLM/NOAA) + CFR parts (33/40/43) and runs basin scoping client-side — functional but a pure-consumer mode with no write path, so the eventual cutover is a known second-build-of-the-enrichment-worker risk that is budgeted, not a surprise. STANDALONE CONTINGENCY: if a chapter commits before DocketClock has its first B2B customer, WW ships a thin standalone that fetches FR + Regs.gov directly and reimplements ONLY the minimum environmental-docket reconciliation (fetch both sources per docket, compare comments_close_on vs commentEndDate normalized to America/New_York, flag Eastern-day disagreement as CONFLICTING, poll FR for extension/correction by docket_id/RIN) — explicitly scoped to ~1 engineer-week, never allowed to grow into a parallel DocketClock — and emits OCD-Event-shaped records from day one so the federal adapter can be swapped for the DocketClock feed later without touching the EIS pipeline, geo-classifier, or action loop. Never block a fundable pilot on substrate readiness; never fork the canonical object schema, only the deployment.

## Geo-scoping — USGS Watershed Boundary Dataset (WBD), HUC-8 as the primary monitoring unit with HUC-12 as an optional finer scope, accessed via The National Map ArcGIS REST WBD Feature Service.

At org setup (one-time, not per-query), a user selects their basin by name search or map click; the system queries WBD to resolve the chosen HUC to its polygon and to enumerate intersecting state FIPS, county FIPS, named tributaries/water bodies, and EPA Region. All of this is frozen into a versioned basin_profile stored in PostGIS. Downstream: state_fips[] scopes the Open States query; named_waters[] + county_fips[] + state_fips[] are the cheap deterministic pre-filter inputs to the Basin Relevance Classifier BEFORE any LLM call; epa_region pre-filters EIS records. The substrate never touches WBD — the only artifact DocketClock ever sees is the resulting huc_8:/huc_12: tag. HUC-8 is the right granularity: fine enough to be meaningful to a local coalition, coarse enough that federal dockets naming a basin or tributary system fall inside it. First basin: Chesapeake Bay (Choptank, Patuxent, Susquehanna, Potomac, James HUC-8 subbasins).

**Gotchas:** (1) The National Map ArcGIS REST is free/keyless but rate-limited and can be slow on complex polygon queries during interactive basin-picker sessions — cache basin_profile after first resolution; never call WBD on the alert hot path. (2) Geo-RECALL (does a docket MENTION/affect this basin?) is a text-classification problem, NOT a geo-query problem — do not conflate WBD point-in-polygon with relevance scoring; the classifier is where recall is won or lost. (3) Named-water matching alone causes BOTH false positives (a USACE 'Chesapeake Bay Harbor deepening' notice that is actually a Baltimore Harbor dredge outside the Choptank HUC-8 — the classifier sees 'Chesapeake Bay' and fires) AND false negatives (an EIS describing an upstream project by COUNTY/PROJECT name with no named tributary — the named_waters filter misses it entirely). Mitigate by running county/state intersection + project-description matching in the pre-filter, and by tuning toward recall with member 'report a missed notice' correction. (4) Large multi-state basins like Chesapeake (6 states, dozens of HUC-8 subbasins) produce a high irrelevant tail at the basin scale — scope alerting to the org's SPECIFIC HUC-8 subbasins, not the whole bay, or the volunteer org will distrust a 40%-irrelevant feed. (5) WBD boundaries are revised periodically; store wbd_version in basin_profile so a boundary change triggers an explicit re-scope rather than silent drift.

## The monitor → act → see-impact loop

1. **MONITOR** — The basin feed surfaces all in-basin windows from three sources (DocketClock-federal, EIS adapter, Open States) by coverage tier, each carrying window_type, basin_relevance (confirmed/probable/possible), days_remaining, the verbatim resolved_close_display (never a collapsed UTC string), confidence, and govinfo_url or the EPA EIS link as the authoritative source. Lead-time reminders fire at 14d/7d/48h/same-day for Tier-1. DocketClock CONFLICTING/STALE honors conflict-dual-fire: closing-soon is SUPPRESSED and a needs-verification notification fires instead with both source observations + govinfo_url — suppression is never silence. EIS-only windows (no cross-source) carry an explicit 'EIS clock — verify at EPA for amendments' label, and any FR-detected EIS extension flips the window to CONFLICTING with a side-by-side. Tier-2 state hearings are informational with an explicit 'verify with the committee before acting' gate before any reminder.
2. **UNDERSTAND** — Each in-basin window gets a plain-language basin brief (Haiku, generated async at classification, regenerated on window.changed): what the rule/EIS proposes, why it matters to THIS basin (citing the classifier's matched_water_bodies/CFR-parts evidence), the operative deadline (verbatim display), how to file, and what evidence is most persuasive for this docket/EIS type. Brief is labeled with confidence and is explicitly NOT a legal opinion; links govinfo_url (federal) or the EPA EIS filing + lead-agency NEPA contact (EIS).
3. **ACT** — Comment composer prompts for the user's position + supporting evidence (water-quality data, site observations, scientific references, local knowledge) and pre-fills basin context (water body names, HUC codes, impact language from the brief). For eligible federal Regs.gov windows: structures the draft and files via the Regulations.gov submitter API IF the Week-1 spike confirms it is open; otherwise degrades to draft + copy-paste-into-portal with a manual filed-confirmation. For EIS windows: directs to the lead agency's stated NEPA submission channel (email/portal) — and the UI states UP FRONT that EIS filing produces a second-class link-out receipt, not a verified Regs.gov submission ID. Drafts saved locally before submission; org admins can see member drafts to coordinate a coalition response before individual filing.
4. **SEE IMPACT (honest procedural receipts only)** — On filing: receipt shows filed timestamp, Regs.gov submission ID (where applicable; absent for EIS link-outs — stated honestly), period_open_at_filing (boolean vs resolved_close_utc), comment_count_at_filing (totalNumComments), and — when a final rule links back — a draft-to-final linkage framed strictly as 'the agency addressed these topics in the final rule that draft comments raised', never 'your comment caused this.' Org dashboard aggregates receipts for grant reporting (windows monitored, comments filed this quarter, dockets participated in). The honest frame — 'you participated, on time, in a real federal process' — holds regardless of outcome and is the receipt the dossier requires. The sideways DocketClock confidence feed (in-basin CONFLICTING windows) is licensable to newsrooms/nonprofits as a separate read-only signal (deferred until DocketClock's public /conflicts feed is stable).

## Coverage tiers (the load-bearing honesty device)

- **Tier-1: Automated, reliable (the core promise)** — Federal EIS notices (EPA EIS database, 45/30 statutory clock with EIS-vs-FR extension reconciliation) and federal-rulemaking dockets (DocketClock: Regs.gov + FR, fully reconciled) that the classifier rates confirmed or probable for the org's specific HUC-8 subbasin(s). Deterministic, verifiable deadlines; DocketClock confidence HIGH/MEDIUM (or EIS clock with explicit amendment-uncertainty when an extension is unresolved). Automated lead-time reminders. Comment filing automated where Regs.gov submission is available. _(label: Badge: 'Verified federal deadline' with DocketClock confidence (High/Medium) OR 'EIS 45/30-day clock'. govinfo_url or EPA EIS link on every record. Disclaimer on every record: 'Watershed Watch is not a legal authority — verify the official notice before relying on this deadline.' The promise is bounded and explicit: 'You will not miss a federal EIS or major rulemaking in your basin during its open comment window — we do NOT systematically monitor local permits.')_
- **Tier-2: Best-effort automated (downgraded, opt-in actionable)** — State environmental LEGISLATION hearing schedules (Open States v3) for basin states, and federal dockets the classifier rates 'possible' (passed pre-filter, low LLM confidence). State bill dates shift without notice; Open States coverage varies by state. Possible-tier federal windows may be tangential to the basin. _(label: Badge: 'State bill — dates may change' or 'Possible basin relevance' at LOW confidence. EXPLICIT user-facing distinction (per critique): 'State LEGISLATIVE hearing — this is NOT a state permit comment period (MDE/DEQ permits are not covered).' No automated push for state windows; a 'hearings require direct verification' gating step fires before any reminder — a volunteer must not travel to testify on a stale date. Possible-tier federal windows collapsed by default.)_
- **Tier-3: Community-reported signal (DEFERRED past MVP)** — Member-submitted local notices (planning-board postings, newspaper legal notices, posted-stake permits) via the NoticeNail pattern, geocoded to HUC-12. NOT verified by Watershed Watch — community intelligence, not systematic monitoring. Deferred; described in the UI as a planned future feature so members understand local coverage is member-dependent and best-effort, never a promise. _(label: DEFERRED. When shipped: 'Reported by a member — not independently verified. Deadline may be inaccurate; confirm with your local agency.' No confidence model, no automated reminders, no filing integration. This is the honest answer to the local-long-tail mismatch: the most emotionally charged fights live here and the architecture cannot systematically surface them — say so plainly in onboarding, not after a disappointed member.)_
