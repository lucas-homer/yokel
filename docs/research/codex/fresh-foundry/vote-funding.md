## Rationale by survivor

**1. Pharmacy Docket Desk — 8.0.** This has the strongest sustainability profile because independent pharmacies face direct reimbursement, audit, controlled-substance, Medicaid, and scope-of-practice consequences, and the credible payers already aggregate buyers: PSAOs, buying groups, wholesalers, and state associations. Willingness-to-pay is plausible if the product is a member-specific regulatory action file rather than another newsletter. Required changes: stay channel-led, start with one state plus CMS/DEA/FDA, use expert review, source citations, and disclaimers; avoid direct SMB acquisition until a channel commits.

**2. Childcare Rule Radar / CareNotice Counsel — 7.6.** The payer is credible through childcare associations, CCR&R networks, shared-services alliances, and funders, while direct provider subscriptions are weaker because operators are exhausted and budget constrained. Distribution is good if bundled with compliance training or association benefits, and trust is compatible if human-reviewed and framed as operational impact rather than legal advice. Required changes: one state, one provider type, association-led buyer, post-adoption compliance briefs, and clear cost/license-risk reduction.

**3. CommentWindow Registry / DocketClock — 7.4.** The payer exists among associations, newsrooms, civic-data teams, and vertical rule-radar products that need deadline infrastructure, but the buyer category is somewhat fragmented and may require design-partner education. Sales cycle is better than B2G if sold as API/SLA infrastructure, and trust compatibility is high if provenance and correction workflows are central. Required changes: federal-only MVP, paid SLA/webhooks/bulk export, deadline confidence labels, change logs, human review for contested deadlines, and no premature state/local promise.

**4. CareRule Scout / Benefits Plainwatch — 7.2.** The need is high-stakes and trusted intermediaries are credible payers, especially P&A orgs, legal aid, disability/aging nonprofits, small provider associations, and foundations. However, budgets may be grant-dependent, procurement can be slow, and direct-to-family monetization would be risky and low-trust. Required changes: sell to navigators/intermediaries, focus on one program/region, use expert-reviewed calm summaries, no eligibility determinations, and avoid mass recipient alerts until validated.

**5. RecordReady Clerk Intake — 6.8.** Clerks and agency coordinators are credible payers because the 72-hour packet-prep crunch is operational and recurring, but municipal sales and official-record risk create a slower cycle. Distribution is plausible through agenda-management consultants or open-government pilots, though trust must be earned with a staff-review workflow. Required changes: one Legistar jurisdiction, one mailbox/body, immutable audit trail, export into existing workflows, measurable staff-time savings, and no autonomous official-record decisions.

**6. Tenant Displacement Docket — 6.6.** The anchor payer is credible through legal-aid, tenant unions, housing foundations, council discretionary funding, and nonprofit partners, but WTP is likely grant/seat constrained rather than durable SaaS. Distribution and trust are good when mediated by tenant-defense organizations, while misuse/privacy constraints limit broader commercialization. Required changes: one tenant-protection-rich city, one case type, anchor partner, access controls, human-reviewed alerts, no landlord-facing or public vulnerable-building intelligence, and proof that alerts arrive early enough to intervene.

**7. Conditions Keeper / Outcome Graph — 6.4.** This is useful civic accountability infrastructure, but the payer is less urgent than for pre-deadline or compliance-risk products; likely buyers are local journalism grants, watchdog nonprofits, clinics, neighborhood councils, or municipal transparency contracts. Sales cycle can be manageable for nonprofit pilots, and trust compatibility is good if it only reports source-linked receipts rather than claims causality or enforcement failure. Required changes: one planning commission, track continuances/votes/appeal deadlines/condition text only, human-reviewed receipts, and package as an add-on to alert/action products where possible.

**8. Concern Router / Civic RouteMap — 6.0.** The workflow is real for navigators, reporters, hotlines, foundations, and legal-aid desks, but payer urgency is diffuse and accuracy risk makes procurement cautious. Distribution is plausible through professional navigator networks, yet willingness-to-pay may depend on grants or embedding inside existing civic products. Required changes: professional-only MVP in one metro plus federal/state/local sources, conservative confidence, citations, human escalation, and make “no reliable path found” a normal trusted output.
