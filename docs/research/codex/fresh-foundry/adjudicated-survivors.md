# Debate gauntlet step 3 — Neutral adjudicator

Verdict key: **survive** = ready for ensemble voting with only normal MVP discipline; **revise** = send forward only in the narrowed form specified; **defer** = keep on shelf until a funded anchor/data proof appears; **kill** = do not advance as a standalone candidate.

## 1) Candidate-by-candidate adjudication

### 1. CommentWindow Registry / DocketClock — **SURVIVE**
- **Decisive evidence:** Feasibility called federal-only MVP **green** because Federal Register, Regulations.gov, and Mirrulations provide real source foundations. Product called it **green** for professional civic operators because missed deadlines destroy trust and downstream tools duplicate this plumbing.
- **Why not just revise:** Red-team’s correctness/liability critique is decisive against state/local and broad SLA claims, not against the federal deadline-object wedge.
- **Must remain:** Federal-only, professional/API infrastructure, provenance/confidence/change logs, human review for contested deadline changes.

### 2. RecordReady Clerk Intake — **SURVIVE**
- **Decisive evidence:** Product demand called it **green**: concrete 72-hour pre-meeting crunch, recurring staff-owned workflow, and clear wasted-effort reduction. Feasibility found one Legistar jurisdiction + one mailbox technically bounded.
- **Main caveat:** Official-record custody and security/procurement are serious, but the human-review packet-prep framing directly answers the red-team attack.
- **Must remain:** Staff workflow, not autonomous record authority.

### 3. Consensus Comment Room — **DEFER**
- **Decisive evidence:** Red-team found the astroturf/AI-laundering critique strikes the core mechanism; product called it **red as standalone** and only yellow as an embedded module.
- **Why deferred, not killed:** Steelman and feasibility support a narrow comment-quality workshop for a trusted organizer with manual filing and public provenance.
- **Trigger to revisit:** A funded association/nonprofit campaign explicitly wants evidence-backed comment synthesis and accepts dissent/provenance constraints.

### 4. Affected-Party Verifier / Notice Compact — **DEFER**
- **Decisive evidence:** Feasibility says address/radius verification is possible, but red-team’s legitimacy critique is decisive: verification can become exclusionary civic infrastructure and suppress renters/unverified speakers.
- **Why deferred:** Needs governance, privacy, and institutional acceptance before it is a product bet.
- **Trigger to revisit:** Neutral planning/legal-aid pilot where verified status is explicitly annotation-only, aggregate-only, and never a gate.

### 5. Concern Router / Civic RouteMap — **REVISE**
- **Decisive evidence:** Feasibility and product both rate it yellow: real navigator pain, but broad concern-to-lever routing creates severe false-positive risk. Steelman survives only as a professional navigator/API tool with “no reliable path found” first-class.
- **Why advance:** The whitespace is real and demand can be recurring among navigators/reporters/hotlines if scope is narrow enough.
- **Revision required:** One metro + federal + one state + one Legistar city/county; trained navigators only; conservative confidence; citations; human escalation.

### 6. NoticeNail / Lot Line — **DEFER**
- **Decisive evidence:** Feasibility rates it yellow leaning red: OCR is easy, but timely official case/deadline verification is the product. Red-team’s false-security critique is decisive.
- **Why deferred:** Strong local story, weak standalone reliability until a paid QA operation and city-specific proof exist.
- **Trigger to revisit:** One city partner funds human verification and accepts “community-sourced signal, not comprehensive monitoring” positioning.

### 7. Tenant Displacement Docket — **REVISE**
- **Decisive evidence:** Product rates it **green if anchored by legal aid/tenant-union workflow** because missed windows can mean irreversible displacement. Red-team and feasibility both warn source data may be too late and misuse/privacy risks are severe.
- **Why advance:** Human stakes and partner-mediated demand are strong enough if the product is not public landlord-usable intelligence.
- **Revision required:** One tenant-protection-rich city, one case type, one anchor tenant/legal-aid partner, strict access controls, human-reviewed alerts, no public vulnerable-building intelligence.

### 8. Conditions Keeper / Outcome Graph — **REVISE**
- **Decisive evidence:** Feasibility supports one planning commission with Legistar/manual extraction; steelman says strongest as post-action/next-window receipts. Red-team is decisive against broad condition-enforcement or causality claims.
- **Why advance:** Useful receipt/outcome infrastructure with moderate demand, especially when paired with alert products.
- **Revision required:** Start with continuance dates, appeal deadlines, votes, and source-linked document-diff receipts; do not claim enforcement/noncompliance or “your comment mattered.”

### 9. CareRule Scout / Benefits Plainwatch — **REVISE**
- **Decisive evidence:** Product rates it **green narrowly scoped** because Medicaid/HCBS/benefits changes are high-stakes and trusted intermediaries have recurring need. Red-team’s panic/legal-advice critique is decisive against direct-to-recipient mass alerts.
- **Why advance:** Strong mission-critical pain and plausible foundation/org channel if expert-reviewed.
- **Revision required:** Expert-reviewed navigator/P&A/legal-aid intelligence for one program/region; no eligibility determinations; no direct mass alerts until validated.

### 10. Childcare Rule Radar / CareNotice Counsel — **REVISE**
- **Decisive evidence:** Product rates it **green through associations**; feasibility says one state/provider type is feasible. Red-team’s buyer-exhaustion critique is decisive against direct standalone SaaS.
- **Why advance:** Clear license/cost/compliance pain and association/CCR&R/shared-services channel can solve trust and billing.
- **Revision required:** Association-led, one state, one provider type, human-reviewed operational impact and post-adoption compliance briefs.

### 11. Pharmacy Docket Desk — **REVISE**
- **Decisive evidence:** Feasibility rates it green-yellow and product rates it green if channel-led: manageable one-state + federal sources and stronger willingness to pay than most civic candidates. Red-team’s incumbent-channel critique is decisive.
- **Why advance:** Commercially plausible if it augments trusted associations/PSAOs rather than competing as another newsletter.
- **Revision required:** Channel-led member-specific regulatory action files, one state + CMS/DEA/FDA, expert-reviewed and source-cited; kill direct one-by-one acquisition unless channel validation is strong.

### 12. Schoolyard Radius — **KILL as standalone**
- **Decisive evidence:** Product calls it red/yellow and commercially fragile; red-team’s anti-housing mobilization risk is central, not incidental. Feasibility also warns relevance/noise may fail.
- **Why kill rather than defer:** Its useful pieces belong inside mitigation-focused land-use/safe-routes partnerships, not as a standalone alert product.

### 13. Open Issue Escalator — **DEFER**
- **Decisive evidence:** Feasibility rates it yellow leaning red because clustering 311 is easy but real public levers may rarely exist. Product warns users may learn the product only documents powerlessness.
- **Why deferred:** Could work only by starting from a known upcoming lever, not the complaint firehose.
- **Trigger to revisit:** A council/public-advocate/department partner names one recurring issue category with predictable budget/procurement/hearing conversion.

## 3) Required revisions to survivors

- **CommentWindow Registry:** Federal rulemaking only; no state/local MVP; deadline uncertainty/staleness are first-class; public provenance, change logs, correction workflow, and human review for contested deadlines.
- **RecordReady Clerk Intake:** One Legistar jurisdiction, one mailbox, one meeting body; human approval before record packet; immutable audit trail; export into existing clerk workflow; no autonomous official-record decisions.
- **Concern Router:** Navigator-facing only; one metro + federal dockets + one state + one Legistar city/county; conservative confidence; citations; “no reliable path found” as normal output.
- **Tenant Displacement Docket:** One city and one case type; anchor legal-aid/tenant-union partner; organization-mediated outreach; no landlord-facing/public vulnerable-tenant intelligence; prove alerts are early enough to intervene.
- **Conditions Keeper:** One planning commission; track continued dates, votes, appeal deadlines, and condition text presence/changes only; no enforcement or causality claims; source-linked human-reviewed receipts.
- **CareRule Scout:** Expert-reviewed navigator/intermediary product; one program/region; no direct-to-recipient mass alerting; calm risk framing, citations, confidence, and legal-advice boundaries.
- **Childcare Rule Radar:** Association/CCR&R/shared-services as customer; one state/provider type; focus on license/cost/compliance impact and post-adoption briefs, not generic civic alerts.
- **Pharmacy Docket Desk:** Association/PSAO/buying-group distribution; one state + federal CMS/DEA/FDA; member-specific action files; expert review and conflict/legal disclaimers.

## 4) Killed/deferred list

### Deferred
- **Consensus Comment Room:** Valuable as a trusted-campaign module, but standalone demand and astroturf perception are too weak.
- **Affected-Party Verifier / Notice Compact:** Geospatially feasible, but privacy/gatekeeping risk requires neutral governed pilot before productization.
- **NoticeNail / Lot Line:** Compelling local signal, but false-security and human-QA burden require funded one-city proof.
- **Open Issue Escalator:** Needs a known recurring public lever first; otherwise likely becomes a frustration dashboard.

### Killed as standalone
- **Schoolyard Radius:** Too likely to become anti-housing/low-relevance mobilization and too commercially fragile. Salvage only as mitigation-focused functionality inside a trusted district/Safe Routes or land-use workflow.
